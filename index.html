Overview

Alan Mathison Turing (June 23, 1912 - June 7, 1954) was an English mathematician, computer scientist, among other things. He is considered to be the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.

Career Known Fors

  • Cryptoanalysis of the Enigma
  • Turing's proof
  • Turing's test
  • The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis

Awards

The Turing Award is recognized as the "highest distinction in computer science". Some also call it the "Nobel Prize of Computing. From 2007 to 2013, the award was accompanied by a prize of $250,000. Effective 2014, the funding level has been increased to $1 million, i.e. four times the previous amount.
